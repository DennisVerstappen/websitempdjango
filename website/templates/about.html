About the project

Monkey Parliament is a project started 8 years ago by me, Dennis Verstappen. After playing guitar for years, I discovered that I also could make music by means of a computer. I started with in-the-box synthesizers and samplers inside several DAWs to create songs instead of only guitar riffs and partitions. Quickly, the guitar became just an instrument which I sometimes featured in my tunes while composing electronic music took over. The urge to get all of my musical ideas out of my head and into my computer resulted in more than 50 releases through YouTube and countless ideas still floating around on my hard disk. Furthermore, the project resulted in several collaborations with producers, singers and musicians.

Art, visuals and movies have been a huge inspiration for ideas and compositions. To support the music releases, imagery and visuals were created that resembled the atmosphere of the tunes. Although, creation of these visuals take even more time than composing music, they became an equally important part of the project. The interest in combining visuals with music resulted in several live visual sets.
